Where it started

The manifesto

I am 42. I am 185 cm tall, and I weighed 131 kg — 290 pounds. This is my last real chance to turn it around.

I'm not here to complain. But I've hit a hard limit: either I take control of my life right now and change it completely, or I face a short one, full of chronic sickness. There is no third option.

How did I get here? Years of zero exercise. No consistency. Junk food and heavy pastries. And every evening, washing down a mountain of workplace stress with beer or wine. I let my guard down, and it piled up — one careless day at a time, over decades in an office chair.

That life ends now. I made a solemn promise to my wife that I will change. I want to be there, in good health, for my three daughters for a long time — and I want to live to see my grandchildren grow up.

So why a coin? I love crypto. I built $290LBS first and foremost for myself — as a metric I can't argue with, a way to carve my transformation into the blockchain forever, where it can't be edited, hidden, or quietly forgotten. I want to fix my life, and set a real example for my daughters. The time has come.

I hold zero percent of the supply. No hidden wallets. No team tokens. No pre-allocated treasury. I have no interest in dumping on anyone. Every single burn is funded directly from my own pocket, with my own SOL. Pure discipline. No estimates. Just math and sweat.

Manifesto 1.1 — after Week 5

Five weeks in, I've learned what I can actually sustain — and I'm tightening one rule to match. From now on, the active calories my watch's Move metric records count ten times over in the burn. Effort under load — real sweat, a climbing heart rate — should weigh more than simply racking up steps. Everything else stays exactly as it was: a pound lost still burns one million tokens, sleep still multiplies the Sweat Burn, and every burn still comes out of my own pocket.

I also want to recognize the people who show up alongside me. From time to time I may reward the most active voices in the community — those carrying the message on X — with a token of thanks. It's not a prize draw and not a payout for sending money: just my way of saying thank you to the people who push this forward.

Five weeks in: down almost 8 pounds, over 8 million tokens gone forever, 80 pounds to go. It will be slow. There will be bad weeks — my knee already forced me to skip one weigh-in, and I showed you that too. I kept the rules modest on purpose, because I intend to keep this promise for years, not weeks.

No shortcuts. Proof of Effort. And I won't stop.
